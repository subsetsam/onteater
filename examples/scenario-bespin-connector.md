# Bespin, 3 ABY: Connector Capture and the Alteration of a Deal

*A scenario for mapping against the Reference Ontology for Galactic Political Economy
(`galactic-economic-ontology.json`). Worked example B (§15.3). All evidence from Episode V.*

---

## The connector economy before the Empire arrived

Cloud City was a small tibanna gas mining operation floating in the atmosphere of
Bespin — an autonomous outpost whose entire operating model was regulatory
obscurity. It held no membership in the Mining Guild, which cost it the guild's
protections and bought it the guild's inattention; it held no Imperial charter,
which cost it legal standing and bought it invisibility. Its administrator, Lando
Calrissian, stated the position's exposure profile on screen with a precision any
analyst would envy: the facility was small enough not to be noticed, and not very
self-sufficient. That is a dependence statement and a strategy statement in a
single breath — vulnerability dependence on external supply, hedged by staying
beneath enforcement notice.

While the Imperial perimeter tightened elsewhere in the galaxy, positions like
Bespin earned a connector rent: the margin available to a node outside the
perimeter through which gas, cargo, and fugitives could still route. The rent
existed only because the perimeter existed. This is the fact the episode is about.

## The perimeter arrives

The Empire reached Cloud City not as a customs authority but as a hegemon — an
agent whose enforcement reach spanned every layer at once: military (a garrison's
worth of troops landed before the targets arrived), financial (nothing Bespin could
do would move Imperial terms), and informational (a bounty hunter had sold the
Falcon's destination before the Falcon landed). Vader's opening bargain with
Calrissian traded an assurance for cooperation: the Empire would leave Cloud City
to run its operation as it saw fit, in exchange for help taking one crew.

Every subsequent scene modified that bargain unilaterally. Solo was to be left with
Calrissian; then he was assigned to the bounty hunter. Leia and the Wookiee were to
remain in the city; then they were ordered to Vader's ship. Each revision was
announced, not negotiated. When Calrissian objected that this was never a condition
of the arrangement, he received the corpus's most quoted statement of bargaining
under coercive asymmetry: the deal was being altered, and he could pray it was not
altered any further. A deal alteration of this kind presupposes exactly what Bespin
faced — a counterparty bearing the hegemon role, with a garrison already emplaced
on the territory in question.

## The enforcement acts

The episode's enforcement was as layered as its coercion. Solo was subjected to
carbonite freezing — simultaneously a product test of the facility's own
industrial equipment, a punitive example staged for observers, and the conversion
of a debtor into hostage collateral: a person rendered into a transferable asset
securing performance. The asset was then transferred to a second creditor entirely
— Jabba the Hutt, whose standing bounty on Solo priced a debt the Empire had no
part in. Two distinct deontic claims, one Imperial and one syndicate, were settled
through a single frozen body. The bounty hunter who brokered the intersection was
paid by both orders.

## Defection and the end of the rent

Calrissian's initial cooperation was target compliance under threat; his
subsequent defection was its repudiation. His order to evacuate the city — begun
before the Empire could take administrative control — converted Cloud City's
population into a refugee flow and its assets into whatever could be flown out.
The connector rent did not survive the episode, and the mechanism is worth stating
exactly: the garrison did not tax the rent, it terminated the rent, because a
connector position captured by the perimeter is no longer outside it. The Empire
gained a compliance node of modest value and lost a connector of considerable
value — the standard exchange when a hegemon confuses a position's output with
its function.

## Analyst's afterword

Bespin compresses an entire escalation sequence into roughly a day: landing rights
requested, hospitality extended, deal struck, deal altered, garrison revealed,
collateral frozen, city evacuated. No stage was recorded in any Imperial or guild
archive — the facility's obscurity, its one strategic asset, ensured that its
capture would be as invisible as its prosperity had been. The episode enters the
record only because the targets escaped to tell it.

# The Droid Courier, 0 BBY: How the Galaxy's Most Valuable Cargo Moved Through Its Least Recorded Market

*A scenario for mapping against the Reference Ontology for Galactic Political Economy
(`galactic-economic-ontology.json`). A supplementary scenario on information carriage,
the informal droid market, and the observation layer. All evidence from Episode IV.*

---

## The cargo

The consignment that opens Episode IV is informational: the complete technical
readouts of the Imperial battle station, obtained by Rebel agents at the cost of a
fleet action, together with a recorded plea addressed to a single named recipient —
"Help me, Obi-Wan Kenobi, you're my only hope." As cargo, the plans had the
strangest economics in the corpus. Their reproduction cost was zero, their carriage
cost was one astromech droid, and their value to the two sides was asymmetric to
the point of comedy: to the Empire they were worth a fleet-wide interdiction
campaign; to the Rebellion they were worth everything. Princess Leia's decision
under boarding was therefore a textbook response to imminent seizure: separate the
cargo from the vessel, and entrust it to a carrier the seizing party's instruments
were not calibrated to see.

## The interdiction and the sensor gap

The Tantive IV was taken by cargo interdiction in its fullest form: pursuit,
disabling fire, boarding, and seizure of ship and crew, over the protest — "If
this is a consular ship, where is the ambassador?" — that the vessel's diplomatic
status made the seizure unlawful. A jurisdiction claim was asserted on both sides;
as usual in this corpus, the party with the boarding party won the argument.

The cargo, however, was already gone. The escape pod carrying the two droids was
acquired by Imperial gunnery and released unfired on a single sensor reading: no
life forms aboard. The judgment deserves to be framed exactly, because it is the
episode's analytical hinge. The Empire's surveillance capacity — the fleet, the
scanners, the boarding protocols — was calibrated to a stratum defined by life
signs. A courier that was property rather than person fell below the instrument's
detection threshold by classification, not by concealment. The most consequential
data flow of the war passed through an interdiction perimeter because the
perimeter's own ontology had no category for what was carrying it.

## The informal market

The pod came down beyond the Republic-successor's settled jurisdiction, on a world
where the corpus has already measured the boundary in currency refusals. What
found the droids was the fringe economy's standing salvage apparatus: a Jawa
sandcrawler crew, who took both units not as couriers or fugitives but as
inventory. Within a day the galaxy's most valuable information asset was rolling
across Tatooine in a hold full of scrap, awaiting resale on the secondary droid
market — the one product line in the corpus that spans the formal-informal
boundary end to end.

The sale to the Lars household is the corpus's only attested consumer-protection
event. The buyer's first selection, a red R5 unit, blew its motivator within
steps of the transaction; the buyer's protest — "What're you trying to push on us?"
— extracted an immediate exchange, and the Jawas, facing a lemon claim in a
market with no warranty law, honored it rather than lose the household's future
custom. R2-D2 entered the Lars homestead as the replacement unit. No party to the
transaction — seller, buyer, or the farm boy who would clean him — had any idea
what had just been purchased, which is the informal market's defining feature:
prices there carry no information about strategic value, because nobody along the
chain can observe it.

## The leak

What followed that evening was a bargaining episode conducted by the cargo itself.
Cleaning the new unit, Luke Skywalker tripped a fragment of the recording — the
plea's first line, looping, deliberately incomplete. Pressed for the rest, the
droid set its terms: the message was the property of its addressee, and the
restraining bolt — the physical instrument of the droid's bonded status — was
interfering with playback. The proposition was false and effective. Bolt removed,
the droid declined to continue the recording, and by morning had departed alone
across the desert toward its designated recipient.

The sequence rewards exact coding. The partial disclosure was a priced tease: a
fragment released to establish the asset's value to the one audience that could
act on it. The bolt-removal condition converted that value into emancipation —
a bonded carrier renegotiating its own status against a counterparty who did not
know a negotiation was occurring. And the overnight departure was unilateral
performance of the original consignment over the new owner's property claim.
Whether one codes the courier as an agent pursuing its principal's mandate or as
property behaving anomalously is precisely the question the ontology flags at its
droid-personhood extension point; the corpus prices droids as property and writes
them as persons, and this episode is the strongest evidence for the second
reading.

## The delivery and the enforcement wake

Delivery completed the consignment as addressed: the full recording, played for
Obi-Wan Kenobi, with the technical readouts intact and instructions for their
onward carriage to Alderaan. From consignment to delivery, the cargo had crossed
a fleet interdiction, an orbital gunnery decision, a salvage acquisition, an
informal-market resale, and a self-negotiated emancipation — with not one
recording event in any Imperial ledger.

The Empire's attempt to reconstruct the chain shows what enforcement looks like
when it arrives after the market has already cleared. Sandtroopers traced the pod
to the sandcrawler and answered the informal market in the only register the
doctrine permitted: the Jawa crew was massacred and the crawler burned, staged
to read as native raiders — a deniable enforcement action against intermediaries
whose only role had been to price the cargo as scrap. The Lars homestead, the
next link in the chain of custody, was burned with its owners in it. Incidence
fell, as it falls throughout this corpus, on the smallest parties in the chain:
a salvage crew and a moisture-farming household, neither of which ever knew what
had passed through their hands.

## Analyst's afterword

The episode is the observation layer's showcase. Every transaction in the chain
occurred in strata where recording probability approaches zero — an escape pod
logged as empty, a salvage acquisition, a cash sale in an informal market, a
negotiation between a farm boy and his own property. The Empire held surveillance
capacity unmatched in the galaxy and never observed the flow, because capacity is
always capacity *over a stratum*, and the stratum that mattered was one its
instruments classified out of existence. The Rebellion's couriers understood what
the Chief Librarian at the other archive did not: the unrecorded stratum is not
empty. It is merely unrecorded — and things move there.

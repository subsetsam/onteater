# Alderaan, 0 BBY: The Demonstration That Refuted Its Own Doctrine

*A scenario for mapping against the Reference Ontology for Galactic Political Economy
(`galactic-economic-ontology.json`). Worked example C (§15.4). All evidence from Episode IV,
with the doctrine's fiscal prehistory from Episodes II–III.*

---

## The doctrine

The measure cannot be understood apart from the doctrine it was built to
demonstrate. At the conference table aboard the battle station, Grand Moff Tarkin
announced two things in one breath: that the Emperor had dissolved the Imperial
Senate permanently, and that the regional governors now had direct control over
their territories. Asked how the Empire would maintain control without the
bureaucracy, he stated the doctrine in a single sentence: fear will keep the local
systems in line — fear of this battle station.

Read as political economy, this is a proposed substitution of capacities. The
Senate's dissolution liquidated the last of the Republic's institutional capacity —
the machinery of representation, deliberation, and legitimate enforcement that had
been eroding since the emergency powers vote two decades earlier, each grant
formally temporary, none reversed. In its place the doctrine offered terror
capacity: compliance secured by demonstrated willingness to destroy. The battle
station itself was the doctrine's balance sheet — a megaproject of continuous
appropriation, decades in construction, large enough to distort the Empire's own
fiscal posture, and never once shown passing a budget review.

## The credibility problem

A deterrent of this kind has a known defect: it deters only if the willingness to
fire it is common knowledge, and willingness cannot be demonstrated by possession.
Tarkin's solution was the oldest in the coercion literature. A demonstration
target was selected for maximal signal: Alderaan — a founding member world,
peaceful, without a garrison or a fleet, and home to a senator already in Imperial
custody.

The selection was staged as a bargaining scene. Princess Leia was presented with a
demand — reveal the location of the Rebel base — under an explicit conditional
threat to her homeworld. She responded with an act that reads as concession:
naming Dantooine. The concession was false, and the sender fired anyway. Tarkin's
stated reason — Dantooine was too remote to make an effective demonstration —
concedes the measure's actual objective on screen: not extraction of information,
not coercion of the prisoner, but signal to third parties. The demand was
theater; the demonstration was the policy.

## The measure and its incidence

The destruction of Alderaan is the corpus's limit-case instrument. Its
characteristics vector is degenerate at every coordinate that ordinarily makes
statecraft a matter of dials: reversibility zero, granularity a single switch,
targeting resolution an entire inhabited world, deployment lag the length of a
superlaser charge. Incidence fell on a population of a founding member world in
its entirety; no treasury, no government, no distributional decomposition
survives to be tabulated. The ontology records it as an instrument because the
corpus shows it priced, scheduled, and fired as one.

## The effects ledger

The doctrine's own evaluation horizon closed within days, and every line of the
ledger ran against it. The intended effect — overcompliance through fear across
the local systems — is attested only as expectation. The realized effects are
attested as events. The rally effect arrived first: the demonstration that was to
end resistance became the Rebellion's best recruiter. The norm erosion was
self-inflicted and total: a government that destroys its own founding member has
announced that membership protects nothing, converting every loyal world's
calculation from allegiance to hedging. And the autarky drive followed as it
always does: targets of interdiction and terror do not return to the network —
they build hidden bases, dispersed supply, and an economy the sender cannot see.

Four days after the demonstration, the battle station — the doctrine's entire
capital stock, its credibility, and its author — was destroyed at Yavin. The
measure in force did not lapse and was not withdrawn; it was forcibly terminated
with its enforcement apparatus, the termination pathway this corpus runs on. No
successor doctrine replaced the substitution thesis. The Empire simply began
building a second battle station, which is not a revision of the theory but a
refusal to mark it to market.

## Analyst's afterword

Alderaan is the cleanest natural experiment the corpus offers on the question of 
whether terror capacity substitutes for institutional capacity or merely liquidates 
it on a delay. One reading of the evidence is available to any coder: the Empire 
spent two decades and a fiscally distorting megaproject to purchase an instrument 
whose single use produced the coalition that destroyed it. Fear kept the local 
systems in line for four days. The committee is not aware of a shorter half-life 
anywhere in the literature.
